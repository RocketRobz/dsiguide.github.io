    Downgrading (Ugopwn)

    If you need help, ask the Nintendo Homebrew Discord!.
    I worked hard on this guide! If you want, you can donate through PayPal or Bitcoin!

    We will now start downgrading to 1.4 system firmware.

    You MUST have a compatible version of Flipnote Studio in order for this to work.

    Doing anything incorrectly MAY result in a brick. Be careful and follow the guide EXACTLY to prevent bricking.
    THERE IS A HIGH CHANCE OF BRICK WITH THE CURRENT METHOD. YOU ARE RESPONSIBLE IF YOUR CONSOLE BRICKS. PROCEED WITH CAUTION.

    What you need

    • The latest release of Flipnotehax/Ugopwn (USA). No other regions available at this time. In order to download, you will need to search Google for it.
    • The latest release of fwTool
    • The latest release of HxD
    • The latest release of DSi SRL Extractor
    • The latest release of No$GBA
    • Codes.txt
    • The latest release of TWLTool
    • The latest release of NUSDownloader (with dsikey.bin generator)
    • The latest release of OSFMount

    Instructions

    Section I - Dumping the Necessary Files
    1. Power off your device
    2. Insert your SD card into your computer
    3. Copy the contents of ugopwn.zip (It might be named flipnotehax.zip) to the root of your SD card
    4. Copy the contents of FWTool.zip to the root of your SD card and rename FWTool.nds to 2222.nds
    5. Remove the SD card from your computer and insert it into the SD card slot on your DSi
    6. Power on your device
    7. Go to Settings > Data Management > Flipnote Studio > Copy > Yes and then exit to the Home Menu.
    8. Find the application named "Flipnote Studio" and open it.
    9. Press View Flipnote and then press SD Card
    10. Press Select Folder, then User, then ugopwn.
    11. Select the first Flipnote and press Details
      12. , then Edit
    12. "Copy" the first frame
    13. Exit the Flipnote, and proceed to open and press Editon the second one.
    14. On the second Flipnote, in the frame menu, press right on the D-Pad (if right handed) or the A button (if left handed) until a new page is made.
    15. Press the Paste button exactly 122 times
    16. Press the Erase button, followed by the Paste button.

      17. NOTE: IF YOUR DSi FREEZES AT THIS POINT, YOU MUST REPEAT STEPS 8-16 AGAIN! MAKE SURE YOU HAVE FWTOOL NAMED AS 2222.nds!

    17. Select the options Dump CID and Dump nand_dsi.bin. This may take a while.
    18. When both dumps are done, power off your device.
      19. Section II - Downgrading your NAND
    19. Plug your SD card from your DSi into your PC
    20. Open your SD Card's drive and navigate to Private > DS > Title > 4B475545.bin and drag it to the same directory as dsi_srl_extract(make sure that you unzipped dsi_srl_extract.zip!)
    21. Drag 4B475545.bin onto extract srl.bat
    22. Wait, and, when prompted, press any key to continue.
    23. You should see some new files.
    24. Open HxD.exe and select the "Open" option
    25. Find the file named OUT.footer (in your dsi_srl_extract folder) and select "Open"
    26. You should see two blue lines of numbers and letters, one going up and down, and one going left to right.
    27. Find the number "00000380" on the up-and-down line, and find "0F" on the left-to-right line. When you look at where they both lead, you should see two characters. Highlight them.
    28. Look to the right, and you should see a number that is also highlighted, followed by 15 other numbers below. Save all 16 of those (Not the pairs of letters and numbers!) to a text file to use for later. (This is called your Console ID)
    29. Open CID.bin in HxD (CID.bin is located in a folder of your SD Card with random letters and numbers!)
    30. Copy all the pairs of letters and numbers and paste it into a text file. (This is your CID, different from Console ID.)
    31. Open Codes.txt and replace "CID Here" and "ConsoleID Here" with your CID and ConsoleID! Make sure to delete the spaces of the CID! DO NOT DELETE THE [BRACKETS]. Save Codes.txt
    32. Open the directory of your SD Card, the folder with random letters and numbers, and copy nand_dsi.bin to the folder named twltool-v1.6 (make sure that you unzipped twltool-v1.6.zip!)
    33. In the folder named twltool-v1.6, right click in an empty space and select "Open command window here." Then paste in Code 1 (from our new Codes.txt) and press enter!
    34. When done, you should now have a file named NAND_DEC.bin.
    35. Go to Google and search for "DSi Common Key", look in the NeoGaf forum, the common key is the link that they included on the post, minus the .com, of course. Copy it.
    36. Open dsikey.bin Generator.exe from the NUSDownloader folder, and paste it in when prompted.
    37. Open NUS Downloader.exe and check the box that says "Create Decryped Contents (*.app)"
    38. Select: Database > System (With the picture of a DSi) > System Menu (Launcher) > USA > v512
    39. Select "Start NUS Download!"
    40. When done, select: Database > System (With the picture of a DSi) > Nintendo DS Cart Whitelist > All > v256
    41. Select "Start NUS Download!"
    42. When done, select: Database > System (With the picture of a DSi) > System Settings > USA > v512
    43. Select "Start NUS Download!"
    44. Install and open OSFMount
    45. Click the "Mount new..." button, followed by the "Image File" tick mark.
    46. Press the button with the three periods (...) and select NAND_DEC.bin
    47. Select "Partition 0" when prompted. This file should be around 200mb.
    48. Uncheck the box that says "Read-Only Drive", and press OK.
    49. Go to the "My Computer" directory, and you should see a new drive. Double-click it.
    50. Delete anything inside of the "title\0003000f\484e4845\content" directory.
    51. Open the folder called "titles" in the NUSDownloader_v19_DSi directory of your PC, open the folder named "0003000f484e4845", and open the folder named "256."
    52. Copy "00000001.app" and "tmd.256" from the folder you are now in, and paste them in the "title\0003000f\484e4845\content" folder on your new drive in your computer.
    53. Rename "tmd.256" to "title.tmd."
    54. Delete anything inside of the "title\00030017\484e4145\content" directory of the new drive.
    55. Open the folder called "titles" in the NUSDownloader_v19_DSi directory of your PC, open the folder named "00030017484e4145", and open the folder named "512."
    56. Copy "00000002.app" and "tmd.512" from the folder you are now in, and paste them in the "title\00030017\484e4145\content" folder on your new drive in your computer.
    57. Rename "tmd.512 to "title.tmd".
    58. Delete anything inside of the "title\00030015\484e4245\content" directory of the new drive.
    59. Open the folder called "titles" in the NUSDownloader_v19_DSi directory of your PC, open the folder named "00030015484e4245", and open the folder named "512."
    60. Copy "00000002.app" and "tmd.512" from the folder you are now in, and paste them in the "title\00030015\484e4245\content" folder on your new drive in your computer.
    61. Rename "tmd.512 to "title.tmd".
    62. Go to your OSFMount window, and press "Dismount". Exit OSFMount.
    63. In the folder named twltool-v1.6, right click in an empty space and select "Open command window here." Then paste in Code 2 (from our new Codes.txt) and press enter!
    64. When it is done, you should see a file named "NAND_ENC.bin" in the "twltool-v1.6" folder, simply rename this file to nand_dsi.bin. (if it asks you to overwrite, you may want to move the other nand_dsi.bin somewhere else and try renaming ir again.)
    65. Move the new nand_dsi.bin to the folder in your SD card with random letters. If it asks to overwrite, say yes.
      66. Section III - Installing Your Downgraded NAND
    66. Repeat steps 8-16 (for running ugopwn)
    67. Once in FWTool again, select Restore nand_dsi.bin. This may take a while, do NOT EXIT FWTool until the restoration is complete!
    68. Exit FWTool. You should now be completely downgraded to version 1.4! You are ready to install RocketLauncher and HiyaCFW.
